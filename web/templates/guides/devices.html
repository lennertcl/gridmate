Field Explanations

How Devices Work

Each device has exactly one primary type that determines its main function (e.g. washing machine). In addition, a device may have any number of secondary types that add extra capabilities. There is no restriction on which types can be used as primary or secondary — any device type can serve in either role.

Device Name
A friendly name shown throughout the UI (e.g., “Heat pump”, “Volkswagen ID.4”).
Primary Type
The main category of the device. Any device type can be chosen as primary.
Secondary Types
Additional capabilities added to the device. Any device type can be added as a secondary type. Examples:
  • Washing Machine + Energy Reporting Device + Deferrable Load → tracked power usage, optimized scheduling
  • Electric Vehicle + Battery Device + Energy Reporting Device → battery level tracking and consumption monitoring
  • Home Battery + Battery Device + Energy Reporting Device → battery level tracking, charge/discharge monitoring
  • Electric Vehicle + Home Battery → EV treated as a home battery for vehicle-to-grid scenarios

Device Types

Each device type defines a set of parameters that become available when the type is assigned to a device, either as primary or secondary.

Washing Machine / Dryer / Dishwasher

Household appliances with no type-specific parameters yet. Add Energy Reporting Device to track consumption and Deferrable Load to enable optimized scheduling. Optionally add Duration Reporting Device to show remaining cycle time and Automatable Device for remote control.

Electric Vehicle
Range Sensor
HA entity reporting estimated driving range (km, optional)
Charging State Sensor
HA entity reporting if the vehicle is currently charging (optional)

Can be combined with Battery Device for SOC tracking and Energy Reporting Device for consumption monitoring.

Charging Station
Max Charge Rate (kW)
Maximum charging rate (kW, optional)
Session Energy Sensor
HA entity reporting energy used in current session (kWh, optional)

Can be combined with Energy Reporting Device and Deferrable Load for smart charging.

Water Heater
Temperature Sensor
HA entity reporting current water temperature (°C, optional)

A good candidate for Deferrable Load to heat water during cheap energy hours.

Electric Heating
Temperature Sensor
HA entity reporting current temperature (°C, optional)
Thermostat Entity
HA climate entity for thermostat control (optional)
Heat Pump
Temperature Sensor
HA entity reporting current temperature (°C, optional)
COP Sensor
HA entity reporting coefficient of performance (optional)
Home Battery

A stationary home battery system for energy storage. All battery-specific settings are configured as parameters on the device.

Optimization Enabled
Whether this battery participates in EMHASS optimization (default: off)
Max Charge Power (kW)
Maximum power the battery can absorb when charging
Max Discharge Power (kW)
Maximum power the battery can deliver when discharging
Mode Select Entity
HA select/input_select entity to switch the battery operating mode (optional)
Charge Efficiency
Charging efficiency factor between 0 and 1 (default: 0.95)
Discharge Efficiency
Discharging efficiency factor between 0 and 1 (default: 0.95)
Min Charge Level (%)
Minimum battery state of charge to maintain (default: 20%)
Max Charge Level (%)
Maximum battery state of charge allowed (default: 80%)
Target SOC (%)
Target state of charge for optimization (default: 80%)

Typically combined with Battery Device for SOC tracking and Energy Reporting Device for charge/discharge monitoring.

Battery Device
Battery Level Sensor
HA entity reporting battery state of charge as a percentage (required)
Max Capacity (kWh)
Total usable capacity of the battery (required)
Power Sensor
HA entity for charge/discharge power — positive = charging, negative = discharging (optional)
Energy Reporting Device
Power Sensor
HA entity reporting current power consumption (kW, required)
Energy Sensor
HA entity reporting cumulative energy consumption (kWh, optional)
Automatable Device
Control Entity
HA entity (switch/input_boolean) used to start/stop the device (required)
Status Sensor
HA entity reporting current device status (optional)
Duration Reporting Device
Remaining Time Sensor
HA entity reporting remaining operation time in minutes (required)
Deferrable Load

Makes the device available for EMHASS optimization scheduling.

Nominal Power (W)
Power consumption when the device is running. E.g., 2000W for a water heater.
Operating Duration (hours)
How long the device needs to run per cycle. The optimizer finds the cheapest window of this duration.
Constant Power
When enabled, the device always runs at its full nominal power when active (on or off only). When disabled, the optimizer can vary the power between 0 and the nominal power to better match available solar or cheaper time slots. Most household appliances (washing machines, water heaters) should have this enabled.
Continuous Operation
When enabled, the device runs in one uninterrupted block. When disabled, the optimizer may split operation across multiple cheaper time slots.
Earliest Start / Latest End
The allowed operating window. E.g., 08:00–22:00 prevents overnight operation.
Startup Penalty (€)
Cost penalty per start-up to discourage frequent cycling.
Priority (1–10)
Lower numbers are scheduled first when devices compete for the same cheap slots. Default: 5.
